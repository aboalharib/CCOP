# 1 Authority and Ownership

The Climate Change Operation Platform (CCOP) is an independently governed system.

The CCOP Team is the sole authority responsible for the design, development, integration, deployment, operation, and lifecycle management of the CCOP system.

The CCOP Team acts as:

- System Owner
- Technical Authority
- Engineering Authority
- Operational Authority
- Data Authority

No external ministry, regulatory authority, or third party holds decision-making authority over CCOP unless explicitly defined through a future formal agreement.

---

# 2 Governance Structure

## 2.1 CCOP Executive Authority

Responsible for:

- strategic direction
- budget approval
- major architectural decisions
- final approval of major releases

## 2.2 CCOP Program Governance Board

Responsible for:

- oversight of project execution
- risk and performance review
- approval of design reviews
- release authorization

## 2.3 Systems Engineering Authority

Responsible for:

- system architecture integrity
- requirements management
- interface control
- technical baseline control

## 2.4 Functional Leads

The CCOP Team shall include clearly defined functional leadership for:

- software engineering
- hardware / sensor engineering
- communications engineering
- data and analytics
- quality assurance
- configuration management
- risk management

Each lead is accountable for discipline-specific compliance and technical performance.

---

# 3 Engineering Discipline and Reviews

CCOP shall implement structured engineering review gates including:

- System Requirements Review (SRR)
- System Design Review (SDR)
- Preliminary Design Review (PDR)
- Critical Design Review (CDR)
- Test Readiness Review (TRR)
- Operational Acceptance Review (OAR)

Progression between phases requires documented internal approval.

---

# 4 Quality Assurance

CCOP shall maintain an internal Quality Assurance framework ensuring:

- traceability from requirements to implementation
- test validation of all functional components
- calibration and validation of sensor systems
- documentation completeness
- auditability of changes

QA authority shall be independent of development execution.

---

# 5 Configuration Management

All hardware, software, firmware, and documentation shall be subject to formal configuration control including:

- version control
- change requests
- impact analysis
- release baselines
- archive and traceability

No system change shall be implemented without formal review and approval.

---

# 6 Risk Management

The CCOP Team shall maintain an active Risk Management Plan covering:

- technical risks
- operational risks
- security risks
- supply chain risks
- data integrity risks

Risks shall be assessed, tracked, mitigated, and reviewed periodically.

---

# 7 Data Governance

The CCOP Team is the sole custodian of system data.

The team is responsible for:

- data integrity
- data validation
- data lifecycle management
- access control
- backup and recovery

---

# 8 Release and Acceptance Authority

Since CCOP is internally governed:

- acceptance criteria shall be defined internally
- acceptance testing shall be conducted by designated internal reviewers
- final approval authority rests with CCOP Executive Authority

---

# 9 Continuous Improvement

The CCOP governance model shall support:

- periodic performance reviews
- architecture evolution
- capability expansion
- lessons learned integration
