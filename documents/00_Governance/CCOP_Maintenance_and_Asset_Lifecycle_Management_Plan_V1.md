# CCOP – Climate Change Operation Platform
## Maintenance and Asset Lifecycle Management Plan

Document ID: CCOP-ASSET-MGMT-001  
Version: 1.0  
Status: Engineering Baseline  
Owner: Ali Al Harib – Chief Engineer  

---

# 1 Purpose

This document defines the maintenance and asset lifecycle management strategy for the Climate Change Operation Platform (CCOP).

The objective of this plan is to ensure reliable long-term operation of monitoring devices through structured asset tracking, preventive maintenance, and lifecycle monitoring.

---

# 2 Scope

This plan applies to the following CCOP system components:

- TerraNode monitoring stations
- TerraEdge distributed monitoring stations
- TerraAir portable monitoring devices
- environmental sensors
- communication modules
- power systems
- monitoring infrastructure (Zabbix)

---

# 3 Asset Lifecycle Management Objectives

The CCOP asset lifecycle management framework ensures:

- reliable environmental monitoring
- predictable sensor replacement schedules
- maintenance traceability
- system reliability
- reduced operational downtime

---

# 4 Asset Categories

Assets within the CCOP system include:

| Asset Category | Examples |
|---|---|
Monitoring Devices | TerraNode, TerraEdge, TerraAir |

# 6 Sensor Lifecycle Tracking

Electrochemical sensors have limited operational lifetimes.

The system shall automatically track:

- sensor installation date
- expected lifespan
- sensor serial number
- replacement history

Lifecycle metrics include:

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
days_used | operational days since installation |
days_remaining | estimated days before replacement |
life_percent_remaining | percentage of remaining lifespan |
lifecycle_status | sensor health classification |

---

# 7 Lifecycle Status Classification

Sensor lifecycle states are defined as follows.

| Status | Condition |
|---|---|
OK | more than 180 days remaining |
WARNING | 90–180 days remaining |
CRITICAL | less than 90 days remaining |
EXPIRED | sensor lifespan exceeded |

---

# 8 Preventive Maintenance Strategy

Preventive maintenance ensures that sensors and devices remain reliable.

Maintenance tasks include:

- sensor inspection
- filter replacement
- calibration verification
- communication system inspection
- power system inspection

---

# 9 Recommended Maintenance Schedule

| Task | Interval |
|---|---|
Air filter inspection | every 3 months |
Sensor inspection | every 6 months |
Calibration verification | every 12 months |
Full system inspection | every 12 months |

---

# 10 Sensor Replacement Planning

Electrochemical gas sensors typically require replacement every 2–3 years depending on environmental exposure.

Expected lifespan baseline:

| Sensor Type | Expected Lifespan |
|---|---|
Electrochemical gas sensors | ~730 days |
HF sensor | ~547 days |
Ozone sensor | ~547–730 days |

Replacement scheduling should be based on lifecycle monitoring metrics.

---

# 11 Zabbix Monitoring Integration

The Zabbix monitoring system supports asset lifecycle management by monitoring:

- device uptime
- sensor update frequency
- enclosure temperature
- power system health
- communication status
- sensor lifecycle metrics

Alerts generated by Zabbix support early detection of device or sensor problems.

---

# 12 Asset Maintenance Logging

Maintenance actions must be recorded in a maintenance log.

Each record should include:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
device_id | monitoring device identifier |
maintenance_date | inspection date |
technician | responsible technician |
maintenance_action | performed task |
sensor_status | sensor condition |
notes | additional observations |

---

# 13 Failure Handling Procedure

If a device or sensor failure occurs:

1. Zabbix generates an alert.
2. Remote diagnostics are performed.
3. Device health metrics are evaluated.
4. Field inspection is scheduled if required.
5. Repair or replacement actions are performed.

All actions must be documented.

---

# 14 Spare Parts Management

To minimize system downtime, spare parts should be maintained for:

- electrochemical sensors
- particulate sensors
- power modules
- communication modules
- filters and airflow components

Maintaining spare components allows rapid repair of failed devices.

---

# 15 Lifecycle Data Integration

Lifecycle information collected from devices may be integrated into the **CCOP Climate Intelligence Platform (CIP)**.

This enables:

- fleet-wide maintenance dashboards
- predictive maintenance planning
- sensor reliability analytics
- network health monitoring

---

# 16 Continuous Improvement

Operational experience and system monitoring should be used to improve maintenance procedures.

Areas for improvement include:

- sensor replacement strategies
- airflow system design
- communication reliability
- power system durability
