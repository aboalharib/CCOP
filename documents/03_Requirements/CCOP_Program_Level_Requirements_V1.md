# 1 Purpose

This document defines the program-level requirements applicable to the development, governance, deployment, and lifecycle management of the Climate Change Operation Platform (CCOP).

These requirements complement the technical system requirements baseline by defining engineering discipline, governance, quality, deployment, and management controls.

---

# 2 Program-Level Requirements

## 2.1 Program-Level Requirements

### R-1 Total System Responsibility
The CCOP Team shall assume full responsibility for the design, development, integration, testing, deployment, operation, and lifecycle management of the CCOP system.

### R-2 Systems Engineering Discipline
The CCOP Team shall apply structured systems engineering practices across all development phases.

### R-3 Deliverable Control
All hardware, software, documentation, and system capabilities shall be formally defined, version-controlled, and released under configuration management.

### R-4 Operational Capability Support
The CCOP Team shall develop operational procedures, user roles, and readiness processes for system deployment.

---

## 2.2 Sensor Systems Requirements

### R-5 Sensor Equipment Compliance
All environmental monitoring equipment shall meet defined performance, calibration, reliability, and environmental tolerance requirements.

### R-6 Sensor Software Interfaces
Sensor firmware, drivers, and data interfaces shall support structured data acquisition, validation, and secure transmission.

### R-7 Calibration and Validation
All sensors shall be subject to calibration procedures and validation protocols before operational deployment.

---

## 2.3 Communications System Requirements

### R-8 Secure Communications
All communications channels shall support encrypted and authenticated data transmission.

### R-9 Network Monitoring
The CCOP Team shall implement a network management capability to monitor node health, connectivity status, and communication integrity.

### R-10 Redundancy and Resilience
Communications architecture shall support redundancy and failover mechanisms where required.

---

## 2.4 C2 System Requirements

### R-11 Centralized Data Processing
The C2 system shall ingest, process, validate, and store environmental data in structured formats.

### R-12 Real-Time Visualization
The system shall provide real-time dashboards for operational monitoring.

### R-13 Alert and Threshold Management
Configurable thresholds and alerts shall be implemented for anomaly detection and risk notification.

### R-14 Reporting Capability
The system shall support generation of baseline reports, impact monitoring reports, and analytical summaries.

---

## 2.5 Software Engineering Requirements

### R-15 Software Integration
All software components shall be fully integrated across sensor, communication, backend, and dashboard layers.

### R-16 Use of COTS Components
Commercial off-the-shelf software components may be used where appropriate and must be managed under version control.

### R-17 Software Engineering Environment
Development shall be conducted within a controlled environment using version control, testing environments, and documented workflows.

### R-18 Software Release Management
All software releases shall be documented, versioned, and formally approved.

### R-19 Interface Control
All internal and external system interfaces shall be defined and documented.

### R-20 Software Update Management
System updates shall follow formal change control and testing procedures.

---

## 2.6 Systems Engineering and Design Requirements

### R-21 Requirements Traceability
All system requirements shall be traceable to design elements and test cases.

### R-22 Integration Performance
Integrated system performance shall meet defined latency, accuracy, and reliability metrics.

### R-23 Design Reviews
Formal design reviews (SRR, SDR, PDR, CDR) shall be conducted at defined milestones.

---

## 2.7 Human-Machine Interface Requirements

### R-24 User Interface Design
Dashboards and control interfaces shall support intuitive operation.

### R-25 Role-Based Access
User roles and permissions shall be defined and enforced.

### R-26 HMI Validation
Prototypes shall be evaluated prior to final release.

---

## 2.8 Pilot and Deployment Requirements

### R-27 Pilot Project Plan
A structured pilot plan shall define scope, objectives, and success criteria.

### R-28 Minimum Viable Capability
Minimum operational functionality shall be defined and validated during pilot phase.

### R-29 Installation Planning
Installation and site readiness procedures shall be defined.

### R-30 Equipment Installation
All hardware installations shall follow documented procedures.

---

## 2.9 Test and Acceptance Requirements

### R-31 Test Plan
A formal test plan shall define verification and validation activities.

### R-32 Acceptance Criteria
Clear acceptance criteria shall be defined prior to deployment.

### R-33 Operational Validation
System performance shall be validated under operational conditions.

---

## 2.10 Data and Logistics Requirements

### R-34 Data Governance
Data collection, storage, retention, and access control policies shall be defined.

### R-35 Tools and Test Equipment
Field test tools and diagnostic equipment shall be defined and maintained.

### R-36 Spare and Replacement Parts
Spare parts and repair procedures shall be defined for hardware sustainability.

---

## 2.11 Project Management Requirements

### R-37 Project Implementation Plan
A Project Implementation Plan (PIP) shall define phases, milestones, and responsibilities.

### R-38 Progress Monitoring
Periodic internal progress reviews shall be conducted.

### R-39 Project Archive
All project artifacts shall be archived for traceability.

---

## 2.12 Quality Assurance Requirements

### R-40 Quality Assurance Framework
A formal QA framework shall ensure compliance with defined requirements.

### R-41 Auditability
All development and deployment activities shall be auditable.

---

## 2.13 Configuration Management Requirements

### R-42 Configuration Control
All hardware, software, firmware, and documentation shall be under configuration control.

### R-43 Change Management
All changes shall undergo documented review and approval.

---

## 2.14 Risk Management Requirements

### R-44 Risk Management Plan
A Risk Management Plan shall identify and track technical and operational risks.

### R-45 Risk Monitoring
Risks shall be periodically reviewed and mitigation measures updated.
