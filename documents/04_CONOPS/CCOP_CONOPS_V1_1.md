# 1 Purpose

This document defines the **Concept of Operations (CONOPS)** for the Climate Change Operation Platform.

The purpose of this document is to describe how the CCOP system operates in real-world environmental monitoring scenarios and how monitoring stations, communication infrastructure, and centralized platform services work together to support environmental intelligence and reporting.

- CCOP TerraEdge monitoring stations

Environmental monitoring operations require more than isolated sensing devices.

Effective environmental monitoring requires:

- distributed environmental observation
- centralized environmental awareness
- reliable telemetry transmission

The CCOP system addresses these needs through an integrated monitoring and intelligence platform.

# 8 Monitoring Stations

The CCOP monitoring layer includes three monitoring station types.

## 8.1 CCOP TerraNode

A fixed monitoring station equipped with meteorological sensors and environmental monitoring sensors.

Operational role:

- continuous monitoring
- air-quality observation
- meteorological monitoring

---

## 8.2 CCOP TerraEdge

A fixed environmental monitoring station similar to TerraNode but without integrated weather station capability.

Operational role:

- air-quality monitoring
- distributed monitoring deployment

---

## 8.3 CCOP TerraAir

A portable environmental monitoring station designed for temporary or mobile monitoring operations.

Operational role:

- short-duration monitoring
- flexible deployment
- environmental field observation

# 10 Climate Intelligence Platform

The CCOP Climate Intelligence Platform performs the following operational functions:

- telemetry ingestion
- environmental data storage
- environmental analytics
- climate intelligence generation
- environmental reporting support
- dashboard services

The platform aggregates telemetry from distributed monitoring stations and transforms it into actionable environmental intelligence.

# 11 Environmental Reporting Operations

The CCOP platform supports environmental reporting processes by transforming monitoring data into datasets suitable for environmental reporting frameworks.

Reporting outputs may support:

- National Greenhouse Gas Inventory datasets
- Biennial Transparency Report datasets
- Nationally Determined Contribution monitoring
- National Adaptation Plan monitoring
- National Air Quality Monitoring reports

These reporting outputs support national climate governance and environmental transparency.

# 15 Normal Operations

During normal operations:

1. monitoring stations collect environmental measurements
2. telemetry is transmitted to the centralized platform
3. the platform ingests and stores environmental data
4. dashboards display environmental conditions
5. users analyze environmental trends
6. environmental reporting datasets are generated
