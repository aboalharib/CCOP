# 1 Purpose

This document defines the Concept of Operations (CONOPS) for the Climate Change Operation Platform (CCOP).

The purpose of this document is to describe how the CCOP monitoring network operates and how environmental telemetry is collected, processed, and used to support environmental awareness and decision-making.

# 5 Hybrid Operational Architecture

CCOP operations follow a hybrid platform architecture consisting of three operational layers.

```text
Monitoring & Telemetry Layer
Climate Intelligence & Data Layer
Geospatial Operations & Decision Layer

These layers transform environmental measurements into operational environmental intelligence.

6 Monitoring & Telemetry Layer

This layer collects environmental observations from monitoring devices.

Monitoring stations include:

Station	Role
TerraNode	Reference monitoring station
TerraEdge	Distributed monitoring station
TerraAir	Portable monitoring station

Telemetry generated by these devices is transmitted to the backend platform.

7 Climate Intelligence Platform (CIP)

The CCOP Climate Intelligence Platform (CIP) processes environmental telemetry.

CIP operations include:

telemetry ingestion

telemetry validation

sensor calibration workflows

environmental data governance

environmental analytics

alert generation

reporting dataset generation

CIP converts raw telemetry into structured environmental intelligence.

8 Telemetry Processing Workflow

Operational data flow:

Monitoring Stations
(TerraNode / TerraEdge / TerraAir)
        ↓
Communication Network
        ↓
Telemetry Ingestion
        ↓
CCOP Climate Intelligence Platform (CIP)
        ↓
Validation / Calibration / Governance
        ↓
Analytics / Alerts / Reporting
        ↓
Geospatial Dashboards
9 Sensor Calibration Operations

Calibration operations maintain measurement accuracy.

Calibration workflows include:

factory calibration verification

TerraNode reference comparison

calibration adjustment algorithms

Calibration ensures measurement consistency across the monitoring network.

10 Environmental Data Governance Operations

Environmental data governance ensures dataset reliability.

Governance processes include:

telemetry validation

dataset traceability

governance metadata assignment

reporting dataset verification

These processes maintain the integrity of environmental data.

11 Geospatial Operations Layer

The geospatial operations layer provides environmental situational awareness.

Capabilities include:

monitoring station map visualization

environmental overlays

alert mapping

environmental trend visualization

Operators can visualize environmental conditions across the monitoring network.

12 Environmental Reporting Operations

Telemetry processed by CIP is used to generate reporting datasets.

Reporting outputs may include:

air quality monitoring reports

environmental trend summaries

climate monitoring datasets

These reports support environmental decision-making.

13 Monitoring Scenarios

The CCOP system supports several operational scenarios.

Urban Monitoring

Monitoring stations deployed across cities.

Industrial Monitoring

Stations deployed near industrial facilities.

Regional Monitoring Networks

Stations deployed across large geographic regions.

Coastal Monitoring

Stations deployed along coastal areas.

Offshore Monitoring

Stations deployed on offshore platforms.

14 Simulation Operations

Before pilot deployment the CCOP system undergoes simulation validation.

Simulation includes:

simulated monitoring stations

synthetic telemetry generation

calibration workflow testing

governance validation

dashboard behavior testing

Simulation ensures system readiness before real deployment.

15 Monitoring Station Lifecycle

Monitoring stations follow a lifecycle including:

planning

installation

monitoring operations

maintenance and calibration

relocation or retirement
